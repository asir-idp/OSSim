Example: Contiguous memory management with fixed-size partitions (first fit)

Memory is divided into fixed-sized partitions, entire processes are allocated in one of these available partitions. in "first fit" policy, the first partition which is large enough is selected.

Processes are allocated in the table order.

Comments:

  • First-fit policy is fast but not efficient, partitions may be larger than processes and generate much internal fragmentation.
